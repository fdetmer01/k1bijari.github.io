Exploring Boston

Boston can be explored by many different ways: walking, guided tours, biking or also by boat. For a good overview of activities and places to visit, see bostonusa.com.

Below, we have summarized a few of the most popular (and our favorite) activities.


Freedom Trail

The Freedom Trail offers a great way to explore the city by walking (Boston is known for being one of the most walkable cities in the country). You only need to follow the red line marking the trail through the city, which will lead you to all its historical places. The Freedom Trail is very popular and perfect to get to know the city. See also here for more details and the description of the trail.


The State House is one of the first sights on the Freedom Trail.

The red line (bricks on the ground) leads you on the Freedom Trail through the city.

New England Aquarium

The New England Aquarium is great place to enjoy watching all kind of sea life and learn about ways to protect the maritime environment. Furthermore, together with Boston Harbor Cruises, it offers whale watching excursions, starting from Boston Harbor. Visit neaq.org and cityexperiences.com for more information.

Seaport

The Seaport District together with Fan Pier Park is a neighborhood with many dining options and a great view of Downtown Boston. For more information, visit here.

Boston Common and Public Garden

Boston Common is one of the oldest public places in the country. The adjacent Public Garden is a nice park inviting for relaxed strolls or also a Swan Boat ride. See here for more details.

North End

An originally Italian neighborhood with many (Italian) restaurants and historical sites. See here for more information.
