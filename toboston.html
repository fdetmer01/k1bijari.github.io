Travel to Boston


Depending on where you are travelling from, you could come by plane, car, or train. Below you find some information about the different travel options. Please reach out to us with any questions you might have!


Travel by Plane

Boston Logan Airport is the local airport serving many domestic and international destinations. Direct flights from Germany are from Frankfurt or Munich. There are also many options for connecting flights. If your flight route includes a layover somewhere in the US (e.g., New York), please make sure to have enough time between your two flights since you will have to go through immigration at the first airport you arrive at in the US (and this can sometimes take some time).

Once you landed in Boston, you can easily get to the city center with the Silver line (which is a bus line) or the Blue line (metro).

Travel by Car

When traveling from Northern VA, the shortest route passes near Philadelphia and goes through NYC. If you have some more time and would like to do a bit of sightseeing on the way, a nice route avoiding going directly through NYC is via I-270 North to Frederick, MA, continuing on route US-15 N to Harrisburg (if you are interested in history, Gettysburg is a nice stop on the way!), and then via I-78 E, I-287 N to CT-15 N, from where the route will be the same as for the option through NYC. The route via I-78 E is longer, but has overall much lower tolls and might, depending on traffic conditions, actually be faster.

Travel by Train

There are several Amtrak connections to Boston, e.g., from DC (Union Station) or also, once a day, directly from Burke Centre, VA to Boston, South Station.
