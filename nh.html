Hikes in New Hampshire!

New Hampshire has a great variety of hikes to offer in the White Mountains, Lakes Region ,...!

For a good overview of different hikes (from short to long, easy to strenuous hikes) visit, e.g., nhfamilyhikes.com .


Our favorite trails

We had the chance to explore a few hikes in the area. Here is a selection of our favorite ones:


Welch and Dickey Mountains

See here for more details and description of the trail.

This trail is sometimes quite steep (“European-style” switch-backs are not that common here…), but the hike provides great, rewarding views over the White Mountains

For parking at the trail head, make sure you have $5 cash or a check!

Pine Mountain (Gorham)

See here for more details and description of the trail.

Note: We managed to miss the first trail junction and ended up doing the hike backwards, which led to an extremely steep descend – also possible, but not recommended.

We hiked this trail in fall, it will be looking a bit different in May!

Mount Washington (we did not hike it… (yet?))

Mount Washington is the highest mountain in the Northeastern United States (6,288.2 ft or 1917 m).

Different options for getting to the summit of it include hiking (one needs to be well prepared!), driving up the Mount Washington Auto Road, or taking a guided tour with a small van that takes one up to the summit (or the highest possible point depending on weather condition). We did the latter, which was very convenient and allowed us to enjoy great views. For more information, visit here.


The closer one gets to the summit, the colder and windier it gets.

If you are lucky and have good weather, you can see the Atlantic Ocean from the top of Mount Washington (silver band visible at the horizon).

Pine Mountain (Alton)

Nice, easy trail in the Lakes Region with beautiful view of Lake Winnipesaukee. See here for more details and descriptions of the trail.
